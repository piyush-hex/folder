Chapter 8 – Information Security and Cyber Law

Information security is a critical aspect of protecting data and systems from unauthorized access, breaches, and cyber threats. It encompasses a range of practices, technologies, and policies designed to safeguard sensitive information and ensure the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of data. With the rise of digital communication and online transactions, the need for robust security measures has become paramount. Cyber law, on the other hand, refers to the legal frameworks and regulations governing the use of the internet and digital technologies. It addresses issues such as data protection, intellectual property rights, cybercrime, and privacy concerns. Together, information security and cyber law play a vital role in creating a safe and secure digital environment, ensuring that individuals and organizations can operate confidently in the online world.

Questions and Answers

1. What are some activities that are considedered as cyber crime?

Ans: Cybercrime ranges across a spectrum of activities. At one end are crimes that involve fundamental breaches of personal or corporate privacy, such as assaults on the integrity of information held in digital depositories and the use of illegally obtained digital information to harass, harm, or blackmail a firm or individual. These new cybercapabilities have caused intense debate. Pegasus spyware, for instance, according to its creator, the Israeli cyber-intelligence firm NSO Group, is sold exclusively to government security and law enforcement agencies and only for the purpose of aiding rescue operations and battling criminals, such as money launderers, sex- and drug-traffickers, and terrorists. Yet, the smartphone-attached spyware, which can steal private data without leaving an obvious trace of its activities, has been widely used covertly by governments to track politicians, government leaders, human rights activists, dissidents, and journalists. It was even used to track Saudi journalist and U.S. resident Jamal Khashoggi months before his murder and dismemberment by Saudi agents in October 2018. Also at this end of the spectrum is the growing crime of identity theft.

2. What are the major objectives of the ICT Policy 2022?

Ans: The ICT Policy 2022 of Nepal aims to transform Nepal into a digitally advanced society. Its major objectives are:

  • 1. Promote Digital Literacy: Encourage and expand digital literacy to ensure that everyone has access to basic ICT skills.
  • 2. Encourage Innovation and Startups: Foster a digital entrepreneurship ecosystem and support the growth of ICT startups.
  • 3. Enhance Infrastructure: Improve internet connectivity and digital infrastructure across the country, especially in rural areas.
  • 4. Cybersecurity: Strengthen cybersecurity measures to protect citizens from online threats and cybercrimes.
  • 5. Government Digitalization: Promote e-governance to make public services more efficient and accessible.
  • 6. Promote Digital Economy: Facilitate the growth of a digital economy and support the use of ICT in sectors like agriculture, health, and education.
  • 7. Regulate Digital Platforms: Establish regulations and frameworks for managing online platforms, digital content, and data privacy.

3. Explain Cyber Law in Nepal with its Provisions

Ans: Cyber Law in Nepal is primarily governed by the Information Technology Act 2063 (2006), which deals with various aspects of cybercrime, digital transactions, privacy, and intellectual property. The provisions include:

  • 1. Cybercrime Offenses: The law addresses crimes such as hacking, data theft, digital fraud, identity theft, and cyberbullying.
  • 2. Data Protection and Privacy: It includes provisions for protecting individuals’ personal data and ensures that data is processed fairly and securely.
  • 3. Electronic Transactions: The law recognizes the legality of electronic records and digital signatures for electronic transactions, ensuring that online contracts are legally binding.
  • 4. Content Regulation: It provides guidelines for regulating the content that is published or shared over the internet, prohibiting offensive, obscene, and harmful content.
  • 5. Cybersecurity: The law emphasizes the importance of securing information systems and networks against cyber threats and mandates organizations to take preventive measures.
  • 6. Intellectual Property: The act includes provisions for protecting intellectual property rights in the digital domain, preventing the unauthorized distribution and use of copyrighted materials.
  • 7. Penalties: The law sets penalties for various cybercrimes, including fines and imprisonment, depending on the severity of the offense.
